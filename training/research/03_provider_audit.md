# Harassment-prevention training audit — provider scan for TogetherBook

Honest verdict up front: most published material is more confident about its production values than its pedagogy. The UK financial-services-specific stuff (Skillcast non-financial misconduct, Marshalls FCA) is regulator-shaped but **thin on scenarios**. The polished US providers (Traliant, EVERFI/ComplyEQ) have the cinematics but generally **don't speak the FCA's language**. Nobody combines both well. That is the gap TogetherBook should fill.

---

## Provider-by-provider findings

### 1. Traliant — Preventing Workplace Harassment, UK Edition
**URL:** https://www.traliant.com/courses/preventing-discrimination-and-harassment-training-for-the-uk/

- **Duration:** 35 minutes (also marketed at 45 min in the blog post; suggests modular packaging).
- **Style:** "Cinematic" / "Netflix-style" with professional actors at a fictional company event called "The Summit." Notably **no cartoon avatars**.
- **Question approach:** A **"No-Fail Quiz Show"** — scenario-driven, gamified, designed not to punish wrong answers. Good principle, gimmicky branding.
- **Refresh:** Markets itself as "No reruns" — annual content rotation. This is the single best idea I saw across the audit.
- **Mobile/A11y:** WCAG 2.1/2.2 AA, Section 508, 100+ languages.
- **UK fit:** Includes a clickable map of Britain to surface country-specific EEO law. Pitched against the Worker Protection Act 2023 ("reasonable steps").
- **Tone:** Conversational, low patronising-content. Closest to "treats you as an adult" of the polished US providers.
- **Pricing:** Hidden behind size-tiered quote forms (1–24 up to 10,000+).

### 2. EVERFI / ComplyEQ — Global Harassment & Discrimination Prevention
**URL (redirected):** https://complyeq.com/courses/workplace-training/global-harassment-discrimination-prevention/
**Blackbaud case study:** https://everfi.com/case-studies/workplace-training/blackbauds-successful-launch-of-respect-at-work-training/

- **Duration:** 35 minutes.
- **Modules:** Bystander Intervention; Discrimination; Retaliation; Working Across Cultures; Respect, Acceptance, and Inclusion.
- **Style:** Character-based scenarios, interview-style videos with subject-matter experts and real employees, motion graphics for abstract concepts. **Mixed format**, not avatar-led.
- **Question approach:** 2–3 option scenarios with feedback; **forced-progression** ("learners generally must select the correct answer to proceed"). This is the cheap trick the CEO will hate — it rewards persistence, not understanding.
- **Tone:** "Learner-first by design, safe to engage, and built to retain attention." The phrase "*safe to engage*" is good. The "*Now let's meet [name]*" trope is exactly the energy EVERFI's older modules used to deliver, though it's softened in the newer Respect@Work suite.
- **Pricing:** Enterprise-only.

### 3. Skillcast — Non-Financial Misconduct + SMCR Conduct Rules
**URLs:**
- https://www.skillcast.com/courses/smcr-non-financial-misconduct-training-course-training-course
- https://www.skillcast.com/industries/financial-services

- **Duration:** Non-Financial Misconduct microlearning = **15 minutes**. Conduct Rules microlearning = **5 min each** (six rules). Senior Manager scenario courses = 60 min.
- **The crucial sentence:** *"Non-financial misconduct is behaviour that is unrelated to regulated activities, including serious offences such as harassment, bullying, sexual discrimination and sexual misconduct."* Followed by: *"The FCA has indicated it is prepared to consider non-financial misconduct when assessing fitness and propriety."* This is the regulatory hook nobody else articulates this cleanly.
- **Style:** Microlearning; SCORM-only; **no scenario detail published**. The page is a sales brochure, not a syllabus.
- **Question approach:** Not disclosed publicly.
- **Tone:** Dry. Regulator-aligned. Will not delight anyone.
- **Pricing:** Library subscription (~£5–15 pp/year tier signal industry-typical).

### 4. iHASCO — Bullying & Harassment + Sexual Harassment Awareness
**URLs:**
- https://www.ihasco.co.uk/courses/detail/bullying-and-harassment-training
- https://www.ihasco.co.uk/courses/detail/sexual-harassment-awareness-training

- **Duration:** Bullying & Harassment 25 min; Sexual Harassment Awareness 20 min; manager variants ~30 min.
- **Style:** Presenter-led video (Chris Miller fronts the Sexual Harassment module). Practical scenarios, with one redeeming feature: the **"grey areas"** section that explicitly walks learners through situations that may or may not be harassment.
- **Question approach:** **20-question multiple-choice end-of-course test** + in-section questionnaires. This is pure recall, the exact anti-pattern the CEO will reject.
- **Tone:** "Simple and straightforward." Translation: bland. The brand voice is "approachable HR generalist," not "intelligent finance adult."
- **A11y/mobile:** 40 languages (machine-translated — a red flag; we should not use machine-translated DEI content), printable certificates, "nothing to install."
- **Pricing:** From **£5 pp** annual subscription. Cheapest of the field.

### 5. Marshalls (Marshall E-Learning / Ciphr)
**URLs:** https://marshallelearning.com/types/compliance-courses/, https://learningnews.com/news/marshall-acm/2023/marshalls-launches-new-fca-compliance-training-courses-for-financial-services-firms

- **Positioning:** UK-rooted, DEI heritage (founded 2002), now part of Ciphr; 2023 launch of 20-course FCA library covering Consumer Duty, AIFMD, IDD, Solvency II, MiFID II, plus modern slavery, bribery, GDPR.
- **Style:** Not disclosed in detail on public pages.
- **Verdict from public material:** Closest to a UK financial-services-native catalogue, but the harassment material is **not visibly differentiated from generic UK SME content**. They have the regulator lens but appear to bolt it on to the same module everyone else sells.
- **Pricing:** Not published.

### 6. ACAS — free bullying & harassment e-learning
**URL:** https://elearning.acas.org.uk/

- **Duration:** 30 minutes. Free.
- **Style:** "Bullying and harassment 2026" — refreshed annually (good practice). Generic non-sector framing.
- **Tone:** Statutory-body neutral. Useful as a **legal-baseline reference** but never adequate as a single course for a regulated firm. Worth linking to from TogetherBook's module footer for "official sources."

### 7. CIPD / HR Inform
**URL:** https://www.cipd.org/en/topics/bullying-harassment/

- Not a course provider. Provides **factsheets, viewpoint papers, and the manager guide "Dealing with bullying and workplace conflict."** Best used by us as content credibility — link to CIPD guides from inside modules so learners see we cite the professional body, not just our own policy.
- The CIPD line "*culture, policy, training*" is the framing the CEO will accept. Avoids the word "compliance."

### 8. University HR programmes
- **UCL** (https://www.ucl.ac.uk/human-resources/news/2025/nov/new-mandatory-training-all-ucl-staff): mandatory by 31 Jan 2026 in response to Office for Students Condition E6. Single course covering bullying vs harassment vs sexual misconduct distinctions, multiple reporting routes, freedom-of-speech tension, staff-student relationships. **Includes content warnings** — good practice we should adopt. Built on LearnUpon. The framing line is excellent: *"Our ability to deliver world-class teaching and research depends on a culture of trust and respect."* Quote-able pattern.
- **Imperial** (https://www.imperial.ac.uk/admin-services/equality/support-for-staff/training/raising-awareness/harassment/): 2.5-hour facilitated session (online or in-person) by external trainer Brenda Lewis Consultancy. Separate manager track. **Live > async** is the bet they're making.
- **Oxford** (https://edu.admin.ox.ac.uk/harassment-training): A laddered set — *Harassment in HE* (30–45 min e-learning), *Equality & Diversity Briefing*, *Little Things Matter* (four short videos on micro-behaviours), *Responsible Bystander* in-person workshop, *Harassment Advisors* induction (3 hrs). **This laddered structure is the best architecture in the audit.**
- **LSE** (https://info.lse.ac.uk/staff/services/Policies-and-procedures/Assets/Documents/harPol.pdf): "What to do if a student discloses harassment or sexual misconduct to you" sits on the Astute LXP platform. Notable that LSE leads with the **disclosure-receiving skill**, not the don't-do-it message. Mature framing.

### 9. Stonewall — LGBTQ+ workplace programmes
**URL:** https://www.stonewall.org.uk/inclusive-workplaces/lgbtq-workplace-training-events

- 45-min "Celebrating Pride in the Workplace," 90-min "Introduction to LGBTQ+ Allyship," 6-month LGBTQ+ Leadership Programme.
- Membership model (Proud Employers). Live-facilitated, not self-paced.
- Useful as a **specific protected-characteristic lens** TogetherBook should call out by name in the equality module, rather than burying LGBT+ examples in a generic "respect" lesson.

### 10. Reasonable Adjustments / Disability Equality
**URLs:** https://www.in-equilibrium.co.uk/reasonable-adjustments-training/, https://www.thinkneurodiversity.co.uk/reasonable-adjustments-training, https://www.ucl.ac.uk/equality-diversity-inclusion/equality-areas/disability-equality/supporting-staff-reasonable-adjustments-training-line-managers

- Universally **manager-targeted**, not all-staff — which is correct: the legal duty under the Equality Act 2010 sits with the employer/manager.
- In Equilibrium runs case-law and scenario exercises tracking the employee lifecycle (applicant → progression). thinkneurodiversity adds the neurodivergence dimension explicitly.
- This protected characteristic is under-treated in every general harassment module above. TogetherBook should not repeat that mistake.

### 11. Active Bystander framework
**URLs:** https://www.activebystander.co.uk/, https://righttobe.org/guides/bystander-intervention-training/

- The 5Ds (Direct, Distract, Delegate, Delay, Document/Disapprove) are now the **de facto framework** across UK universities and Worker Protection Act-aware providers. Using anything else will look like NIH.

## Patterns that work (confirmed across the audit)

1. **Laddered architecture** — Oxford's stack of 30-min e-learning + 4 short videos + in-person bystander workshop + advisor track is the gold standard. Single 45-min marathon (Traliant, iHASCO, ACAS) is not.
2. **Annual content rotation** — Traliant's "no reruns," ACAS dating its course "2026." Year-2 should not be year-1.
3. **Microlearning for recall + long-form for skill** — Skillcast's 5-min Conduct Rule modules paired with 60-min scenario-led Senior Manager course is the right shape, just executed dryly.
4. **Explicit "grey area" scenarios** — iHASCO's one good idea: a section that forces learners to sit in ambiguity rather than picking obviously-bad behaviour from a list.
5. **Content warnings + an opt-out path** — UCL is leading on this; older US providers ignore it.
6. **Disclosure-receiving as a named skill** — LSE frames this as a discrete competency rather than burying it inside "managers."
7. **Plain-English on top of the legal definition** — Skillcast's non-financial misconduct one-liner is reusable: harassment IS misconduct, misconduct IS a fitness & propriety issue, fitness & propriety IS a regulatory matter.
8. **Bystander as standard non-manager framing** — 5Ds, not "report it to HR."

## Patterns that fail (confirmed)

1. **Forced-progression quizzes** — ComplyEQ/EVERFI "must select correct answer to proceed" rewards click-fatigue.
2. **Pure end-of-module multiple choice** — iHASCO's 20-question recall test. The Western Australian MARS Program study (2024) directly identified this as "tick and flick" and counterproductive: it signals the employer doesn't take risk seriously.
3. **Generic "Now let's meet Sarah" character intros** — older EVERFI/iHASCO/ACAS template energy. Patronising on a trading floor.
4. **Machine-translated content** — iHASCO's "40 languages, machine translated" is a credibility problem for DEI material specifically.
5. **No regulatory hook** — Traliant, EVERFI, iHASCO all under-treat the FCA dimension. For a UK financial group this is the **single biggest gap in the market**.
6. **Stand-alone annual marathon with no spaced refresh** — Imperial's 2.5-hour single session is high-quality but high-friction; learners cram, decay, forget.
7. **"Disclaimer: not legal advice" hedge on every page** — common in Marshalls/iHASCO copy; reads as defensive and condescending. Put one disclaimer in the footer and stop.
8. **No "I'm not sure / I want to talk to someone" route** — only Oxford and LSE surface this prominently. Most providers route everything to "report to HR," which is the highest-friction option.

## Specific anti-patterns called out

- **EVERFI / ComplyEQ:** "*learners generally must select the correct answer to proceed*" — this is the click-through-until-right pattern.
- **iHASCO Sexual Harassment Awareness:** *"presented by Chris Miller … 40-language course"* (machine-translated) — single presenter, machine-translated, recall test. Avoid emulating.
- **Traliant** "**No-Fail Quiz Show**" — the *idea* (low-stakes, no shame) is right; the *branding* (quiz-show, cinematic Summit event with actors) is exactly the dress-up energy the CEO flagged. Steal the principle, drop the styling.
- **Skillcast** Non-Financial Misconduct 15-min Express — the regulatory hook is right but the format is so brisk it can't possibly change behaviour. Use the content framing, not the duration.

## Design implications for TogetherBook

1. **Build a laddered set, not a single 45-min module.** All-staff core (10–12 min) + non-manager bystander module (10 min) + manager track (20 min) + line-manager reasonable-adjustments module (15 min) + senior-manager fitness-and-propriety module (15 min). Year-2 refresher (10 min) that **rotates** scenarios.
2. **Anchor every scenario in our actual contexts.** Open-plan office, broker calls, client lunches, off-sites, returning from maternity, contractor on the trading floor, mortgage-completion-night drinks. Never "Sarah and John in the photocopy room."
3. **Lead the non-manager module with the 5Ds** (Direct, Distract, Delegate, Delay, Document). It's now the UK-university standard; any alternative looks invented-here.
4. **Frame harassment as non-financial misconduct from page one** — link it explicitly to the FCA's fitness-and-propriety regime and the SMC Conduct Rules. This is the differentiator no off-the-shelf UK provider executes well. Quote the FCA framing verbatim once, then translate into plain English.
5. **Use grey-area questions, not recall.** Each scenario presents 3 plausible interpretations, lets the learner pick, then shows what an employment tribunal / FCA enforcement / line manager would each weigh. No "wrong answer locks the page" mechanic.
6. **Add a confidential reflection prompt** (logged to the user's private journal in TogetherBook, never to a score) — "When have you witnessed something that sat oddly with you?" Improves recall and signals seriousness.
7. **Build the disclosure-receiving skill as its own named module** for managers (LSE pattern). Most providers skip this.
8. **Include a content warning + opt-out path** at the start of any sexual-harassment scenario (UCL pattern). Make "I want to speak to someone instead" a first-class button, not a footnote.
9. **Reasonable adjustments gets its own module**, not a paragraph buried in equality. Manager-targeted. Tracks the employee lifecycle (applicant → return-to-work → progression) as In Equilibrium does.
10. **Stop using stock-photo casts and avatars.** Either film named TogetherBook colleagues willing to be on camera, or use illustrated abstract scenarios that don't pretend to be people. Never the uncanny-valley middle.
11. **Plain-English legal box on every relevant screen** — one paragraph, no "this is not legal advice" disclaimer per page. Cite Equality Act 2010, Worker Protection Act 2023, FCA Conduct Rules with hyperlinks to the actual primary sources, not summaries.
12. **Rotate the question bank annually.** Year 1 = trading-floor scenarios; year 2 = remote/Slack/Teams scenarios; year 3 = client-facing/third-party scenarios (the Worker Protection Act 2023 third-party angle the CEO is exposed to).
